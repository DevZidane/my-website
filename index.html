About

Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum), known as the "Queen of Spices," is a valuable crop grown under shaded, humid conditions. It is sensitive to soil conditions, especially drainage. In Ratnapura, cardamom is grown mostly in the hilly, wet zones such as Balangoda highlands, Kuruwita slopes and Pelmadulla riverbanks.

Ideal conditions for growth:

  • Elevation: 600–1200m
  • Soil: Loamy, well-drained, rich in organic matter
  • Shade: 50–70%
  • Annual Rainfall: 2000–4000 mm



This project investigates the impact of waterlogging in cardamom plantations in the Rathnapura district, Sri Lanka. Due to high rainfall and poor drainage, cardamom farmers often face significant challenges that reduce yield and increase plant diseases. This study presents survey-based findings and practical recommendations for improved farm sustainability.

Study Area – Rathnapura

Rathnapura, located in south-central Sri Lanka, receives an average rainfall of 3500–5000 mm per year. The area has hilly terrain, and many plantations are located on slopes or low-lying regions with clayey soils that retain water, contributing to waterlogging issues.

Challenges Identified

However, increased rainfall and poor land drainage often reduce productivity. This platform aims to guide farmers in overcoming these issues.

Why is waterlogging a problem for cardamom?

Waterlogging occurs when excess rainwater saturates the soil, especially in low-lying and clay-heavy areas like Kuruwita and Pelmadulla.

Main Effects are:
  • Root rot due to poor oxygen supply
  • Yellowing leaves and stunted growth
  • Nutrient imbalance in the soil
  • Increased susceptibility to pests and diseases


Most vulnerable times are Southwest monsoon (May–September) and extreme rainfall events.

**Waterlogging not only reduces yield but can kill entire plants if not managed properly**

Solutions & Recommendations

Farmers in Ratnapura can reduce the impact of waterlogging using the following approaches:
  1. Field Design
    2. *Construct raised beds or ridge and furrow systems
    *Ensure slope-based drainage channels to redirect excess water
  2. Soil Improvement
    3. *Mix soil with sand or compost to improve drainage
    *Apply organic mulch to regulate soil moisture and prevent compaction
  3. Planting Techniques
    4. *Use well-spaced planting to avoid root overcrowding
    *Select disease-resistant varieties
  4. Maintenance Tips
    5. *Clean drains regularly, especially before monsoon
    *Monitor water levels and signs of leaf yellowing or wilting

    ***These methods are low-cost and suitable for farmers with limited resources***
